Dear Cucurbita:

Do you really believe in the Easter Bunny?

Signed,
Just Not Sure What to Think

Dear Just Not Sure:

Of course! With the discovery of the ancient scrolls at Nag Hammadi, we finally know the truth! Happy Easter!

And there appeared at that time a rabbit, the size of a man, and he did carry a basket of foil eggs on a bed of coloured straw. And seeing the sepulchre where Jesus lay, and it being Easter Sunday, he rolleth away the stone with a mighty heave and put the basket at the head of the Son of Man. Then hoppeth he from the tomb, and he looketh back three times; but rubbing his back and cursing, he decideth not to roll the stone again.

“Who hath rolled the stone away?” the Roman guards did ask when they awoke from their slumber, and asking thus, they decided it was the Jews; and the Jews decided it was the Romans; and the Christ-followers decided it was the Christ; and the children decided it was a large white rabbit, with nose of pink and tail of finest cotton, because they had peeked even when they had been told not to.

And when Jesus awoke from death and emerged from the sepulchre the basket was gone. “We don’t know wherein lies the basket,” spaketh the Roman guard; but Jesus had not asked about the basket. “It was the Jews,” spaketh the guard. But even so there was chocolate at one corner of his mouth.

Jesus wept.

And suddenly there did appear a rectangular box of blue; and the breadth of it was one cubit and one half; and the height of it was five cubits; and entering the blue box, the white rabbit disappeared, and the blue box did likewise.

And the children were punished with a rod.
