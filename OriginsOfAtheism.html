Dear Cucurbita:

Have there always been atheists?

Signed,
Bertrand Flowers

Dear Bertrand Flowers:

An excellent question to which the answer is a resounding “No!” You see, Bertie (if I may) for several billion years there was not an atheist to be found on planet Earth. Then, about 30,000 years ago, this conversation happened between two of our ancestors:

Ug: Sky God make rain.

Grock: Sky God? Who that?

Ug: Ineffable presence. Creator. Me made in image!

Grock: Ug look like Aunt Trudy, not Sky God. Me think raincloud make rain.

Ug: God first cause. Grock idiot.

Grock: Me think no invisible transcendent supernatural realm. Ug deluded.

Ug: Me have proof God exist. Proof in stone book. Look! Me and Bonk chosen people!

Grock: Sky God no write book! Ug and Bonk write book!

Ug (dropping stone book on Grock’s head): Grock not chosen. Hey, Bonk! Look! Me have first convert!

Bonk: Convert not moving.

Ug: That downside.

Bonk: No problem. When Ug make omelette, he break egg.

Ug: Sky God have special plan for Grock.

Bonk: Special plan to break on wheel and roast in lake of fire for all eternity!

Ug: Me not know what eternity am. Or wheel. Or fire.

Bonk: Oh, me just saying dumb stuff.

Ug: Deep, Bonk. Now me convert others with book!

And so, Bertie, with the birth of theism came the birth of atheism! Like love and marriage, they go together like a horse and carriage! This I tell you, brother – you can’t have one without the other!

Agape! And NonAgape!

Cucurbita
